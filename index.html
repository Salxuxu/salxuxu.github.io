FP Resource Center Redesign

Redesigned the tool hub for better clarity and consistency — transforming a fragmented experience into a unified, intuitive workspace.

Overview

The Problem

The FP Resource Center suffered from fragmented navigation, inconsistent visual design, and an overwhelming information architecture that made it hard for users to find what they needed quickly.

The redesign focused on establishing a clear visual hierarchy, a consistent component language, and intuitive pathways — reducing cognitive load and improving task completion.

Key Improvements

What Changed & Why

🎨
Visual Consistency
Unified color, typography, and component styles across the hub — eliminating the patchwork feel of the legacy design.
🗂
Clearer Navigation
Reorganized information architecture based on user mental models, reducing clicks to key resources significantly.
⚡
Faster Workflows
Surfaced frequently used tools at the top level and introduced quick-access patterns for power users.

Focal Point Library Homepage Redesign

Transforming a static internal homepage into a dynamic dashboard for coaches — making critical resources instantly accessible.

Role
Lead Product Designer
Scope
UX Research · IA · UI Design
Outcome
+35% Event Engagement

The Problem

Understanding the Problem

📋
Lack of User Value
The homepage only displayed a welcome banner and phishing advisory, offering coaches no actionable resources or updates.
🧭
Poor Navigation
Unclear entry points into resources made it difficult for coaches to find what they needed, leading to abandonment.
📉
Low Engagement
The static nature of the site didn't encourage repeat visits. Coaches couldn't see events, newsletters, or library updates at a glance.

Research & Discovery

How I Found the Answers

🎙
User Interviews
Conducted conversations with coaches and stakeholders to identify priority needs and pain points.
🗺
Priority Mapping
Identified core resources coaches needed: events, announcements, newsletters, and training materials.
🏗
Information Architecture
Mapped categories and created a mental model that aligned with how coaches naturally think about resources.
Key Insights
Coaches need quick access to upcoming events and training opportunities
Community recognition and wins drive motivation and engagement
Updates on new tools and training materials need to be surfaced immediately
A scannable, card-based layout enables faster information discovery

The Solution

A Dashboard That Works for Coaches

I redesigned the homepage into a functional dashboard that surfaces the most important resources coaches need, all in one scannable, card-based layout.

📢 Announcements

Highlights company news, conferences, and important updates.

📅 Upcoming Events

Calendar view of trainings, calls, and conferences.

📰 Newsletter Access

Quick entry point to leadership insights and industry trends.

🎉

Wins & Library Updates

Community recognition and new tools drive repeat visits and engagement.

Impact & Results

Measurable Outcomes

Traffic Increase
Post-launch library traffic growth
+35%
Event Engagement
More coaches attending events and trainings
↑
Repeat Visits
Coaches returning for announcements and updates

Reflection

Key Learnings

01
Internal Tools Need Strong UX
Even though the Focal Point Library was built for internal use, applying thoughtful UX principles made a measurable difference in engagement and adoption.
02
Content-Driven Dashboards Drive Engagement
Transforming a static page into a content-driven dashboard with dynamic cards created an experience coaches wanted to return to regularly.
03
Qualitative Research Reveals Hidden Pain Points
User interviews surfaced the motivational role of community recognition — something that wouldn't have appeared in analytics alone.
04
Business & User Goals Can Align
Business objectives — community, training, events — can align with user needs when designed thoughtfully. This project proved that.
